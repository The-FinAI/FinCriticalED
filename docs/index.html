FinCriticalED:

A Visual Benchmark for Financial Fact-Level OCR

Abstract

Recent progress in multimodal large language models (MLLMs) has substantially improved document understanding, yet strong optical character recognition (OCR) performance on surface metrics does not guarantee faithful preservation of decision-critical evidence. This limitation is especially consequential in financial documents, where small visual errors can induce discrete shifts in meaning. To study this gap, we introduce FinCriticalED (Financial Critical Error Detection), a fact-centric visual benchmark for evaluating whether OCR and vision-language systems preserve financially critical evidence beyond lexical similarity.

FinCriticalED contains 859 real-world financial document pages with 9,481 expert-annotated facts spanning five critical field types: numeric, temporal, monetary unit, reporting entity, and financial concept. We formulate the task as structured OCR with fact-level verification, and develop a Deterministic-Rule-Guided LLM-as-Judge protocol to assess whether model outputs preserve annotated facts in context. We benchmark 13 systems spanning OCR pipelines, specialized OCR VLMs, open-source MLLMs, and proprietary MLLMs.

Results reveal a clear gap between lexical accuracy and factual reliability, with numerical values and monetary units emerging as the most vulnerable fact types, and critical errors concentrating in visually complex, mixed-layout documents with distinct failure patterns across model families. Overall, FinCriticalED provides a rigorous benchmark for trustworthy financial OCR and a practical testbed for evidence fidelity in high-stakes multimodal document understanding.

Results Insights

Model Failure Analysis

Representative Failure Cases

✓ Gold Annotation
✗ Model Output

MinerU2.5: MinerU2.5 fails to capture monetary unit signs and introduces noise around mathematical expressions. Dollar signs and currency symbols preceding financial values are dropped, directly degrading Monetary Unit FFA scores. Additionally, mathematical expressions are surrounded by spurious characters introduced during OCR post-processing.

DeepSeek-OCR: DeepSeek-OCR systematically omits dollar signs ($) from monetary values. In the ground truth, three annotated monetary facts ($500,000,000, $21,429,000, and $50,001,000) each include the dollar symbol as a critical monetary unit indicator. DeepSeek-OCR strips these symbols, producing bare numeric strings that lose their monetary unit annotation and directly reduce Monetary Unit FFA scores.

Gemma-3n-E4B-it: Gemma-3n-E4B-it exhibits repetition errors in long-form financial text due to hallucination. The model repeats the last sentence of the fourth paragraph verbatim, a failure mode characteristic of autoregressive models that lose track of their generation context over extended passages.

Llama-4-Maverick: Llama-4-Maverick exhibits column misalignment when processing tables with nested header structures. In the ground truth HTML, the "4. Securities Acquired (A) or Disposed Of (D) (Instr. 3, 4 and 5)" column contains three sub-columns. Llama-4-Maverick completely ignores the sub-column headers and captures only top-level header names, causing systematic column misalignment in all subsequent data rows.

GPT-4o: GPT-4o's output is heavily truncated on this SEC Form 4 document even with increased context window. The model refuses to fully process the financial transaction table, likely due to content safety constraints triggered by dense financial disclosure data. The result is a partial rendering that omits transaction details, prices, and entity information present in the ground truth, leading to systematic drops across all FFA metrics on this document.

GPT-5: GPT-5 exhibits multiple free-form errors on this bond certificate table. The specific certificate rates (4.56% per year for Class A, 4.96% per year for Class B) are hallucinated as the generic label "Fixed rate (per annum)". Precise monetary values for price to public, underwriting discount, and proceeds to transferor are replaced with fabricated placeholders ("See prospectus" or "Not offered") text that does not appear in the source document. A spurious "Summary of terms" heading is also introduced, absent from the ground truth.
